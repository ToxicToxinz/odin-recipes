Ozzie Roll Recipe

  • Large sweet potato: Sweet potato adds color, flavor, and moisture to the rolls.
  • Whole milk: Used to cook the sweet potatoes.
  • Warm water: Warm, not hot, water is key for a good rise.
  • Active dry yeast: Helps give the rolls their rise.
  • Granulated sugar: Balances the flavor of the dough and keeps the yeast active.
  • Large eggs: Binds and adds volume to the dough.
  • Butter: Melted to add richness to the rolls and the topping.
  • Bread flour: High protein content means feathery but hearty rolls.
  • Kosher salt: Enhances the overall flavor.
  • Cane syrup: Combined with butter to brush over the tops of the rolls for a sweet touch.
  • Flaky sea salt: A hint of salt to finish takes these rolls to the next level.
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine all the dry ingredients – flour, salt, sugar, and yeast.
  2. Warm the milk and water and add them to the dry ingredients. Make sure the liquids are not too hot; they should be around the same temperature as your body. Too hot, and it might kill the yeast.
  3. Melt the butter and beat the egg. These should be added to your mixture and everything should be well combined.
  4. This mixture should be kneaded until it forms a soft yet sturdy dough.
  5. Cover the dough and let it rise for about an hour or until it has doubled in size.
  6. Cover the dough and let it rise for about an hour or until it has doubled in size.
  7. Let them rise again for another 30 minutes.
  8. Preheat your oven to 180°C. Once it’s ready, you can place your rolls in there to bake for about 20-25 minutes or until they turn a tantalizing golden brown.
